The problem

Policy exists. Governed operations usually do not.

Current enterprise reality

  • - Policies live in PDFs, manuals, shared drives, and inboxes.
  • - Distributed teams still draft, review, and publish in live channels.
  • - Audit reconstruction often happens after the fact.
  • - Static storage does not equal operational control.

Why it matters

  • - Policy drift creates real supervisory and reputational risk.
  • - Overlapping jurisdiction, product, branch, and user rules are hard to manage manually.
  • - Generic AI tools do not create the governance boundary regulated firms need.
  • - Firms need evidence, approvals, and control before external use.

What it is

Policy Gate is an executable governance core.

High-level operating model
Approved policy becomes governance objects. Governance objects propagate through a workspace hierarchy. The system computes the effective policy state for the specific user, task, product, channel, and jurisdiction. Drafting, review, approval, release, and audit then occur inside that governed boundary.

Product pillars

Four investor-relevant pillars

1. Policy Gate Core
Maintains the authoritative source of truth and converts approved policy into enforceable governance objects.
2. Effective Policy State
Computes the operational policy that actually applies after inheritance, filtering, overrides, and conflict resolution.
3. Compliance Boundary
Constrains AI-assisted and human workflows to authorized sources, disclosures, permissions, and approval paths.
4. Governance Event Chain
Preserves audit-ready evidence from policy creation and propagation through review decision and release authorization.

Moat

Why this can be defensible

Architecture moat
Hierarchy, inheritance, effective policy computation, propagation, approval gating, and evidence preservation form a technical governance system rather than a lightweight workflow layer.
Workflow moat
The value appears where policy governs the actual moment of work: drafting, review, publication, escalation, and supervision.
IP strategy
Visible architecture can be patented while prompts, scoring, extraction heuristics, thresholds, and operational methods remain behind confidentiality and trade-secret controls.
